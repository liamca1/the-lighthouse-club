History of The Lighthouse

Obscure lighthouse famous for its breathtaking panoramic views across lake Plötzensee. Erected in 1969, when free-spirited Dutch seafarers, enticed by tales of Berlin misadventures, arrived to the shores of Plötzensee. Upon entering the turbulent waters of Wedding, they became entranced by a beach full of beautiful bodies expressing themselves freely to enchanting rhythms on a Tuesday afternoon. This other worldly distraction led to the beaching of their vessel (now our DJ booth). With this incident, the Berlin senate requested immediate action to be taken to avoid further similar occurrences. Funding was allocated and the infamous Lighthouse erector Thea-Luz Vesta was hired. Thea-Luz provided the architectural design work that resulted in a cross-functional building with hidden qualities and features that allow for alternative realities to be created, going beyond the building's intended purpose. In more recent times, the adaptation of this space has been supported by interior detailing from the Studio99 design team, and will once again open its doors to the public on 30th October.
